Bad Bones outside golden gate

A boy came to me from across the busy street.

He whispered into my left ear, as I siped dawn whiskey.

Be weary, my dear, of prophets.
Wraped up in silky garments are answers,
To questions without solutions.
Sweet story built jerusalem's walls,
but burnt the temples.

He listened,

and saw my sunken sullen eyes,
and saw my sullen sunken bones,
my words weaker than his whispers.
